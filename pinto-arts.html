History

Pinto Art Museum, located in Antipolo City, was established in 2010 by Dr. Joven Cuanang, a prominent neurologist and art patron, as part of his mission to promote Filipino art and culture. The museum is part of the Silangan Gardens and sits on a 1.3-hectare property surrounded by lush greenery. Its name, “Pinto,” meaning “door” in Filipino, symbolizes the museum as a gateway to contemporary and traditional art. Over the years, it has gained popularity as both an art and cultural hub, showcasing works from various Filipino artists and fostering a deeper appreciation for local art.

Attractions

Pinto Art Museum is renowned for its expansive collection of modern and contemporary art. The museum features various galleries that house paintings, sculptures, and installations. Notable attractions include thought-provoking artworks and interactive pieces that challenge conventional views. The architecture is as captivating as the art itself, with its Mediterranean-inspired design characterized by white-washed walls, open-air spaces, and gardens that seamlessly blend with the indoor exhibits. The museum also includes a theater and café, offering visitors an immersive and holistic cultural experience.

Visiting Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Weekdays offer a quieter experience compared to weekends, and mornings are ideal for better lighting and cooler temperatures.
  • Photography: Visitors are encouraged to take photos, but flash photography is not allowed to preserve the art pieces.
  • Clothing: Wear comfortable attire and shoes as the museum's grounds include stairs and garden paths.
  • Respect the Art: Do not touch any of the artwork, and maintain a respectful distance when viewing installations.

Transportation

Pinto Art Museum is easily accessible from Metro Manila. Visitors can take a bus or jeepney heading to Antipolo City. Once in Antipolo, tricycles are readily available to take you directly to the museum. For those driving, follow Ortigas Avenue Extension or Sumulong Highway to reach the museum. Ample parking is available on site.

Time Open

Pinto Art Museum is open from Tuesday to Sunday, typically from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM. It is closed on Mondays. It’s recommended to check the museum’s official website or social media pages for any updates on operating hours or special events. Entrance fees apply, and cash is generally preferred for ticket purchases.
