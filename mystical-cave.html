History

Mystical Cave, located in Antipolo City, Rizal Province, is a famous natural attraction known for its unique rock formations and spiritual significance. Discovered by Inday Nelly Deles in the 1970s after she claimed to have seen visions of the cave, it soon became a place of pilgrimage and mystery. The cave is said to be imbued with a spiritual aura, drawing both religious devotees and adventure seekers. Over the years, it has become known for hosting religious activities, especially during Holy Week, due to its sacred atmosphere and naturally formed rock shapes resembling religious icons.

Attractions

The Mystical Cave is renowned for its intricate stalactites and stalagmites, some of which resemble figures such as angels, the Virgin Mary, and other religious icons. Guided tours take visitors through a network of chambers, where formations are highlighted with stories and legends shared by knowledgeable guides. Visiting Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Early mornings or late afternoons provide cooler temperatures and a quieter atmosphere.
  • Footwear: Wear sturdy, non-slip shoes, as the cave floor can be uneven and damp.
  • Lighting: Bring a flashlight or headlamp to help navigate darker areas inside the cave.
  • Guided Tours: Opt for a guided tour to learn about the legends and history associated with the formations.
  • Respect the Site: As a place of spiritual significance, visitors are encouraged to maintain silence and avoid touching the rock formations.

Transportation

Mystical Cave is located on the outskirts of Antipolo City. From Metro Manila, you can take a bus or jeepney bound for Antipolo. Once in the city, tricycles are available to take you to the cave site. For those driving, follow the signs along Marcos Highway or Ortigas Avenue Extension, and parking is available near the cave entrance.

Time Open

The Mystical Cave is generally open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily. It's advisable to call ahead or check online for any changes in schedule, especially during the rainy season when tours may be restricted for safety reasons. An entrance fee is typically required, so bring cash for admission and donations.
